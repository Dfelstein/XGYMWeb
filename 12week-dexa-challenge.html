What Makes This Different

Real Results, Real Proof

Unlike typical transformation challenges, every participant in the XGYM 12-Week DEXA Challenge gets a DEXA scan at the start and at the end. No selfies. No guessing. No vanity metrics.

You'll have undeniable, medical-grade data showing exactly how much body fat you lost, how much muscle you gained (or maintained), and precisely how your body composition changed over 12 weeks. This is the gold standard in body transformation measurement—the same technology used in clinical and research settings.

Every participant receives professional coaching, structured training programming, nutrition targets based on metabolic testing, and weekly check-ins. You're not doing this alone. You're doing this with a coach, with a community, and with proof.

What's Included

Your Complete 12-Week Program

Everything You Need

1

Initial DEXA Scan & Coach Onboarding

Week 1 begins with your baseline DEXA scan, RMR test, and full body composition assessment. You'll then connect with your assigned coach to review results and set 12-week targets.

2

Structured 12-Week Training Program

Receive a detailed, periodised training program designed for body composition change. Progressive overload, exercise variation, and strategic volume management throughout the 12 weeks.

3

Nutrition Targets Based on Your RMR

Your daily calorie and macro targets are determined by your measured resting metabolic rate, not generic calculators. These adjust throughout the program as needed.

4

Weekly Check-ins With Your Coach

Every week, connect with your coach to discuss progress, ask questions, adjust your program, and receive accountability and support as you work toward your goal.

5

Final DEXA Scan & Results Debrief

Week 12 concludes with your final DEXA scan. Your coach will review the results, show you exactly what changed, and debrief on your transformation and next steps.

6

Certificate & Community Recognition

Complete the challenge and earn your XGYM 12-Week Challenge Certificate. Top performers are recognised and celebrated on our channels.
