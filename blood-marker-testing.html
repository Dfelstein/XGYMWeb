YOUR BLOOD DOESN'T LIE

Comprehensive blood panel testing for performance and health optimization

What We Test

Comprehensive Blood Marker Analysis

Testosterone & Hormones

Total and free testosterone, DHEA, cortisol levels. Critical for muscle growth, recovery, energy, and mood in both men and women.

Thyroid Function

TSH, Free T3, Free T4. Thyroid dysfunction sabotages metabolism, energy, and fat loss. Often overlooked in training populations.

Iron & Ferritin

Serum iron, ferritin, TIBC. Iron deficiency causes fatigue and poor performance. Athletes and menstruating individuals need regular monitoring.

Vitamin D & B12

25-hydroxy vitamin D, B12, folate. Deficiencies are epidemic and directly impact immune function, energy, and recovery from training.

Inflammatory Markers

C-reactive protein (CRP), homocysteine. Elevated inflammation indicates recovery issues, metabolic dysfunction, or underlying health problems.

Lipid Panel

Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides. Essential for cardiovascular health. Training and nutrition profoundly impact these markers.

Blood Glucose & Insulin

Fasting glucose, insulin, HbA1c. Reveals glucose control and insulin sensitivity—foundational markers for performance and longevity.

Full Blood Count

Hemoglobin, hematocrit, red/white blood cells, platelets. Detects anemia, infection risk, and overall blood health status.

Why Blood Testing Matters

Internal Health Determines External Results

Nutritional deficiencies, hormonal imbalances, and metabolic dysfunction operate silently. You might feel fine while your blood markers reveal serious issues sabotaging your training results and long-term health.

A low-iron athlete will never achieve peak performance. An athlete with vitamin D deficiency has compromised immune function and slower recovery. Elevated inflammation means your body is in a constant state of fighting itself rather than building muscle. Insulin resistance makes fat loss nearly impossible regardless of how strictly you diet.

These problems don't show up in how you feel. They show up in your blood. That's why serious athletes and anyone invested in their health get tested. Data-driven training begins with knowing your internal health status. XGYM partners with professional pathology providers to deliver comprehensive blood analysis, with results reviewed and interpreted by your coach to inform your training and nutrition strategy.

The Process

How Blood Testing Works

Book

Schedule your blood draw appointment. We'll provide pre-test fasting instructions and answer any questions.

Draw

Visit our partner pathology clinic for a quick, professional blood draw. Takes about 5 minutes.

Results

Receive comprehensive results within 5-7 business days. You'll get your values and reference ranges.

Debrief

Meet with your XGYM coach to review results and discuss what they mean for your training and health.

Who Should Test

Blood Testing Is For You If

Poor Recovery

Constantly sore, fatigued, or getting sick easily? Blood markers reveal deficiencies and inflammatory issues preventing recovery.

Low Energy or Mood

Persistent fatigue or mood issues often stem from vitamin D, B12, iron, or thyroid problems that show up in blood work.

Age 35+

Hormonal shifts happen gradually. A baseline blood panel helps catch issues early and monitor hormone trends over time.

Body Composition Journey

Anyone on a serious fat loss or muscle gain mission should know their glucose, insulin, lipid, and hormone levels.

Athletic Performance

Competitive athletes benefit from knowing iron, vitamin D, testosterone, and inflammatory status for performance optimization.

Family Health History

If heart disease, diabetes, or other conditions run in your family, baseline blood work helps establish your personal health status.

Partnership with Professional Pathology: XGYM blood testing is completed through partnership with accredited pathology providers. We coordinate scheduling, result interpretation, and coaching consultation. Your results remain private and secure, shared only with you and your coach.
