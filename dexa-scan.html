What Is DEXA

Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry

DEXA (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry) is a medical-grade imaging technology that provides the most accurate body composition analysis available. Unlike scales, bioelectrical impedance, or visual estimation, DEXA uses low-dose X-ray technology to precisely distinguish between lean muscle mass, body fat, and bone mineral density with accuracy margins of ±1-2%.

Originally developed for bone density assessment in medical settings, DEXA has become the gold standard for body composition measurement in research, athletics, and performance training. It provides regional breakdown of your body composition, allowing you to track muscle gain and fat loss with scientific precision.

At XGYM, we use DEXA scanning as a cornerstone of our performance testing suite. Whether you're tracking muscle gain, monitoring fat loss, preparing for competition, or simply curious about your body composition, DEXA gives you the data you need to make informed decisions about your training and nutrition.

What You Receive

  • Regional Body Fat %: Total body fat percentage plus breakdown by body region (arms, legs, torso, trunk)
  • Lean Muscle Mass by Segment: Exact kilograms of lean mass in each region, showing strength development asymmetries
  • Bone Mineral Density: T-score and Z-score measurements for bone health assessment
  • Visceral Fat Estimate: Measurement of deep abdominal fat linked to metabolic health
  • Android/Gynoid Fat Ratio: Fat distribution pattern assessment relevant to health markers
  • Detailed PDF Report: Professional report with all measurements, graphs, and baseline reference data
  • Coach Debrief: 20-minute consultation to discuss results and plan next steps

Who Needs DEXA

The Ideal Candidate

Strength Athletes

Track muscle gain and ensure your training is building lean mass effectively. Regional data shows if you're developing evenly.

Fat Loss Phase

Verify you're losing fat, not muscle. Many people on calorie deficits lose muscle tissue—DEXA shows exactly what's happening.

Bone Health Monitoring

Track bone mineral density over time. Athletes, especially females, should monitor bone health to prevent osteoporosis.

Competition Prep

Athletes in sports with weight classes or body composition standards use DEXA to optimize their physical preparation.

Health Baseline

Anyone over 35 wanting a health baseline should establish body composition and bone density metrics as reference points.

Fitness Enthusiasts

If you've hit a plateau and want to understand whether your training is working, DEXA provides the evidence you need.
